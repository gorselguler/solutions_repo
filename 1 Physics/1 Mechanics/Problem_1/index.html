ðŸš€ Problem Overview

Investigate how the range of a projectile depends on its launch angle under ideal conditions: - No air resistance - Constant gravitational acceleration

ðŸ“š Theoretical Foundation

The projectile's motion splits into two components:

  • Horizontal displacement: [ x(t) = v_0 \cos(\theta) t ]
  • Vertical displacement: [ y(t) = v_0 \sin(\theta) t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2 ]

Where: - \( v_0 \) = initial velocity - \( \theta \) = launch angle - \( g \) = gravitational acceleration (9.81 m/sÂ²)

Total time of flight when \( y = 0 \):

\[ t = \frac{2v_0 \sin(\theta)}{g} \]

Thus, the range \( R \) is:

\[ R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g} \]

ðŸ§  Key Result: Maximum range occurs when \( \theta = 45^\circ \).
